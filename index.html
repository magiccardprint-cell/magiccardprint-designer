üìè CR80 ID Card Size Information

What is CR80?

CR80 is the most common ID card size and is the same size as a standard credit card. The "CR" stands for "Card, Rectangular" and is part of the ISO/IEC 7810 international standard.

üìê CR80 Dimensions:

  • ‚Ä¢ Width: 3.375 inches (85.6 mm)
  • ‚Ä¢ Height: 2.125 inches (53.98 mm)
  • ‚Ä¢ Thickness: 0.030 inches (0.76 mm)
  • ‚Ä¢ Corner Radius: 0.125 inches (3.18 mm)

üí≥ Common Uses:

  • Employee ID cards
  • Access control cards
  • Membership cards
  • Student ID cards
  • Insurance cards
  • Gift cards

üîß Magnetic Stripe Option:

The magnetic stripe version includes a black magnetic stripe on the back of the card, commonly used for access control systems, time tracking, or payment applications.

CMYK Color Mode is Required

All artwork must be printed in CMYK color profile for our Magicard 300 dye-sublimation printer.

If you submit RGB files:

  • We will convert your file to CMYK without color adjustments
  • Color shifts are expected and unavoidable during this conversion
  • You accept these changes by submitting RGB files

Expected Color Shifts (RGB ‚Üí CMYK)

When converting from RGB to CMYK, these colors typically change:

  • Bright Blues: Become darker or slightly purple-toned
  • Bright Greens & Lime: Become more muted or olive-toned
  • Neon/Fluorescent Colors: Cannot be reproduced‚Äîwill appear significantly darker
  • Vivid Oranges & Reds: May appear darker or less vibrant

üí° Important: Screen colors (RGB) may not accurately represent final printed colors (CMYK). What you see on your monitor will not match the printed card exactly.

Best Practice: Design in CMYK from the Start

To avoid unexpected color changes:

  • Set your design software to CMYK color mode before starting
  • Use CMYK color swatches instead of RGB values
  • Design with print-safe colors (avoid neon or out-of-gamut colors)
